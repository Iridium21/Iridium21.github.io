Iris!

Iris- as we call it at Aeromodelling Club was officially revealed in the tech orientation and the response of the freshies was beyond what I expected. This post will take you through the timeline of building it and my experience of building my first Quadcopter ever. The build, although not tough, was a meditation in itself. I had barely two and a half days to build it and get it flying. We wanted the freshers to see it flying in the orientation. The three months of vacation propelled me towards getting here- and so, I did a lot of research. On the specifications of the parts I would be ordering. Everything is interrelated, and so you have to mentally make sure everything works out exactly how you planned.

Apart from improvisations, one thing I focussed on was achieving a clean build. Space Management was a challenge and fun too. The most time consuming part was cutting wires and soldering them in places to achieve that clean build. Heat shrinks are extremely efficient and satisfying to use. And yes zipties, how could I handle things without them! Those two days were almost spent in TL, I had to go back to my hostel to eat, and that was irritating, given that I live in H6. the cycle rides were terrible, with the rain showers. Me and Jigyasu tested out the Quad in TL, which was quite a risky thing to do. But of course, we had Jigyasu. (Kids, do not try to attempt what Jigyasu does, he has a power to talk with Quadcopters and they seem to obey his commands) The way he controlled Iris, it showed how comfortable he was with Quadcopters. I share similar interests. And I am getting more comfortable with Iris as time passes. The responses are high, it is very sensitive, and that adds to the challenge in flying it. At the end of those two days, Iris was ready, and we were hoping for the best during the Orientation!
